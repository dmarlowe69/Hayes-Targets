The radalt serial output stream is as follows.
 
Two packets of data are transmitted asynchronously, at approx 10Hz, although this rate varies with height and lock status. Each packet contains 8 bytes configured thus :-
 
first packet:-
 
Byte 1      decimal 1
Byte 2      decimal 35
Byte 3      range high byte
Byte 4      range low byte
Byte 5       signal level high byte
Byte 6     signal level low byte
Byte 7     checksum high byte
Byte 8    checksum low byte
 
The checksum is the XOR of bytes 3 to 6 inclusive.
 
second packet
 
Byte 1      decimal 1
Byte 2      decimal 36
Byte 3      raw pressure high byte
Byte 4      raw pressure low byte
Byte 5      gain setting
Byte 6     signal quality indication
Byte 7     checksum high byte
Byte 8    checksum low byte
 
The checksum is the XOR of bytes 3 to 6 inclusive.
 
You need only look at packet one, bytes 3 & 4, which contain the range value. If the unit is out of lock, it sets this value to zero.
Range is scaled 100mm per bit.( 6 times better resolution than analog output!)
Baud rate is 9600, one stop bit, no parity.
